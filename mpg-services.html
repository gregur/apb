Fuel Economy Basics

It's amazing to realize that just one trip in your car often uses as much energy (measured in Joules) as leaving a lamp on for an entire year! With scientists, politicians, and journalists elaborating the implications of climate change and energy security, people everywhere are getting interested in their personal involvement. For manyVolkswagen and Audi owners in the Bay Area, fuel economy is a point of pride and there is a personal interest in fuel economy. Your mechanic should aim to support the conversation. Fuel economy has three main contributors: engine condition, tire condition, and driving styles. Improving Fuel Economy requires attention to the overall balance of these factors along with your own driving styles and expectations of performance in the various conditions you drive in.

Driving Green

Driving green means paying attention to how your own habits fit into the overall economics and culture of driving in our country. We're lucky to live in the SF Bay Area where environmentally-conscious decisions are at the forefront of our public awareness. Look for local interest groups to help address your own needs while maintaining some understanding of the bigger picture. It's best to find an Volkswagen mechanic who sees the logic of moving toward a greener transportation economy, so that you have a partner in the process. Together we can help to be a part of a global solution to environmental crisis.

Saving

Saving money on your car is almost entirely a matter of saving money on fuel. While the IRS estimates 25 cents per mile as the cost of driving, the majority of that figure is the high cost of gasoline, which is here to stay. Find a mechanic who can educate you one-on-one in smart driving practices and share what you learn with our friends and family. A good mechanic can also make technological modifications to your car that will increase fuel economy on every mile.
