Transmission Basics

The transmission is the part of a car that communicates power from the engine to the wheels at a variety of speeds. Whether manual or automatic, a transmission allows the engine to spin at a higher speed than the wheels. The purpose is to translate high speed at lower power (torque) into lower speed at higher power. This is done by mating smaller gears on the engine side with larger gears on the drivetrain side, just like on your bike! A good transmission like those found in Volkswagens requires some care not to damage. Everybody has at some time or another experienced a transmission that has not been properly cared for.

Transmission Repairs

A transmission can be the most expensive part of a car to repair, so it is important to keep your transmission checked regularly by a skilled mechanic. Routine maintenance on the transmission greatly reduces the potential for long-term car bills, so it's a very cost-effective decision. Transmission fluid needs to be periodically replaced, as does the transmission fluid filter. If your car is behaving strangely or jerking about, you definitely have a transmission problem! Fortunately there are ways to revamp a transmission that has been treated improperly. Taking your Volkswagen into a mechanic as soon as you notice problems is the best way to avoid costly repairs in the future.

Transmission Fluid

Transmission fluid simply lubricates all the many complex parts of the transmission which have very high forces applied to them. They don't spin as fast as some parts of the car, such as the turbocharger, but it is imperative to have good transmission fluid at all times so that a small internal defect doesn't become bigger. Fluid burns up over time and can eventually leave the transmission with far too little lubrication. Transmission fluid is the simplest component in the system and the easiest to maintain.
