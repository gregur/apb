Tire Basics

Did you know that tires can make or break your driving experience? We often forget that all the most important aspects of a car, it's weight, its power, and its handling, ultimate rest on tires and that the particular relationship between the tires and the rest of the system can result in a wide variety of driving conditions. Choosing the right tires requires having a clear knowledge of both the intricacies of your Volkswagen's engineering as well your own driving habits and preferences. Since tires are heavily marketed, pick a mechanic who has special experience in Audi and Volkswagen in order to cut through the buzz words and find the tire that delivers what you expect.

Safety

It only takes a moment to understand the importance of properly-tuned tires for your safety: there is truly nothing else in your entire car that is actually touching the earth beneath you. With such a tremendous diversity in tire options today, the cases of accidents involving badly-picked tires are becoming more common. But the "best" tires don't help when they're built for a purpose you're not aligned with; your driving safety requires a close look at your own driving habits and preferences combined with knowledge of the very particular system dynamics of an Volkswagen.

Performance

The experience of performance relies in large part on well-tuned tires for your particular driving style. Some drivers like to learn into corners, while others like to have the freedom to do quick maneuvers through lanes. Volkswagen and Audi have earned a strong reputation for performance-conscious engineering that brings high-tech to the masses. Choosing the right tires for a Volkswagen/Audi requires an intimate familiarity with its design heritage and unusual system characteristics. Good tires don't need to be expensive to radically change your experience of performance; the deciding factor is a careful consideration of what you want it to feel like, in tandem with a mechanic who has a deep understanding of the behavior of new tires.
