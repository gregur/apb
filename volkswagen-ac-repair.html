Air Conditioning Basics

Did you know how complicated your car's air conditioning system is? There are dozens of parts that all must be in top shape to provide that apparently simple experience of cold air on a hot day. Air conditioning also helps remove excess moisture from the Bay Area's most humid days. Air conditioning can often be the difference between driving stressed and driving relaxed.

Air Conditioning Maintenance

Volkswagen recommends that your car's air conditioning be serviced once every two years at least. It's often necessary to check O-rings for cracks and looseness, which can happen to any well-maintained air conditioning system. Particles in the system can cause many of the components to wear out prematurely. The best thing a Volkswagen owner can do to maintain the AC system is to make sure to run the AC on maximum once every few weeks, even if it's cold outside; this will ensure that the system is in top shape.

Warning Signs

If you hear strange noises from your cooling vents, it is likely that the compressor for the system is beginning to fail. If the air is no longer cold, it is probably due to depleted coolant in the system, which often occurs over winter months. If there's a strange smell, some mold or fungus may have grown inside the vent tubes. And if there is moisture coming out of your vents, it is possible that the tube draining the evaporator is clogged. The air should come out quite dry, since an air conditioning system both dries and cools air running through it.
