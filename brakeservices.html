Brake Basics

When you depress your brake pedal, what happens? Fluid is compressed in a pressurized pipe, which flows all the way to the four brake pads, one on each wheel. The brake pads gently rub a special area of the wheel with high- tech materials that absorb the rotational energy while maintaining minimal erosion. Brakes are one of the few systems in modern cars that still rely on simple centuries-old mechanical principles. Brakes ideally should get the most regular service of any component of your car.

Safety

Brakes are paramount in the safety of your everyday commute. One of the most common safety risks in your Volkswagen brake system is the presence of tiny amounts of water vapor. Such a situation occurs whenever you slam the brakes or drive in mountainous terrain. While maintaining brakes are ordinarily considered by even skilled mechanics to be a simple matter of standard protocol, for Volkswagen cars it is important to pay close attention to the driver's own preferences and habits in driving in order to choose brakes that will function properly in the most dangerous moments. Studies show that driver confidence remains the most predictive factor in collisions, so we encourage Volkswagen drivers to find a mechanic who can get you equipped with brakes that make you feel comfortable and in control.

Performance

Choosing performance brakes requires understanding the overall physical dynamics of your car's body, drivetrain, computers, and instruments. These components are the sum total of what you experience when you deftly modulate the brake pedal to hug a turn or slide into a tight spot. The perfect brakes for your driving style need not be expensive; where performance is concerned, it's crucial to talk to your mechanic about a braking system that addresses the interactions of every component of the complex system of your Volkswagen.
