Suspension Basics

The suspension system in your Volkswagen cushions the ride from the bumpiness of the road underneath you. A suspension not only feels more comfortable, it also increases overall driving stability. Like many other essential components of your Volkswagen, it's imperative to keep your suspension well-tuned for the best performance. A faulty suspension can be a safety hazard, and it always makes driving less enjoyable. Suspension is actually one of the most noticeable elements in your driving experience -- in essence everything literally rests on your Volkswagen's suspension. Maintaning a Volkswagen suspension requires knowledge of the entirety of what rests on top of it, not just the integral components.

Shocks and Struts

Both of these components work to dampen the bumpiness of the road. If they are too tight, the wheels of the car can actually leave the ground at high speeds! Shocks are simply springs under each wheel that lessen the amplitude of each bounce the car experiences, and struts are a more complicated version with some extra components. Typically the front end of a Volkswagen has shocks and the rear has struts. Volkswagen's renowned engineering has tuned these components to produce a road feel that can't compare to any other car make, which is one of the most common praise (and complaints!) heard by VW drivers.

Warning Signs for Shocks and Struts

Sometimes when shocks and struts are not in good condition, the driver will experience a bottoming-out effect, where the car dips and actually hits the pavement! This is typically the result of having an unusual amount of weight in the car, whether passengers or cargo. Unpredictable steering and a feeling of rolling around are common symptoms of faulty suspension too.
