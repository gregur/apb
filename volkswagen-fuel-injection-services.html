Fuel Injection Basics

Fuel Injection is one of the greatest feats of modern car engineering. The fuel injector allows you to drive more comfortably with a more reliable driving feel, while using less fuel and helping the environment. Your Volkswagen's fuel injector allows it to get a lot more power from the same amount of fuel as well as the great feeling of a responsive gas pedal! Fuel injection eliminates the need for a carburetor because it sprays fuel directly into the engine cylinders using high-performance nozzles.

Fuel Injection Science

Fuel injection replaced carburetors in most cars in the late 1980s. A carburetor was essentially a more complicated version of the same system. A fuel injector takes gasoline and sprays it through a very tight nozzle, producing a mist directly into the combustion cylinders of the engine. Because this involves fewer moving parts, it's more efficient and less likely to be damaged by ordinary use, but over time a number of problems can develop.

Fuel Injection Problems

There are numerous problems that occur with a fuel injection system. Because the system gets so hot during use, it is not uncommon for fuel injector nozzles to get some burnt debris on them. Dirty fuel injection nozzles can result in a very uneven spray that risks increasing damage over time. A fuel injector can also remain forced open, which causes gasoline to leak into the engine cylinders. If you experience erratic engine power, a choppy idling sound, the smell of fuel, or decreased fuel economy, it's likely that there is a fuel injection problem.
